# Paris Travel Guide

## Culture & Etiquette
- Greeting: Always greet shopkeepers and waiters with a polite "Bonjour" (during the day) or "Bonsoir" (in the evening) before starting a conversation. It's considered rude to skip this.
- Tipping: Tipping is not required in France as service is included (service compris) in the bill. However, leaving 1-2 Euros for good service is appreciated.
- Dining: Parisians dine late. Lunch is usually from 12 PM to 2 PM, and dinner starts around 7:30 PM or 8 PM. Avoid rushing your meal; waiters will not bring the bill until you ask for it ("L'addition, s'il vous plaît").

## Food Recommendations
- Croissants & Pastries: Boulangerie Utopie in the 11th arrondissement has award-winning baguettes and croissants.
- Bistro: Chez L'Ami Jean for classic Basque-inspired dishes, especially their famous rice pudding.
- Fine Dining: Le Comptoir du Relais in Saint-Germain-des-Prés is famous for its gourmet bistro cuisine.
- Local Specialty: Try Steak Frites, Duck Confit, and Escargots.

## Local Attractions & Hidden Gems
- The Louvre: Book tickets in advance. Enter via the Porte des Lions entrance rather than the Pyramid to avoid long lines.
- Eiffel Tower: Best view is from Place du Trocadéro.
- Hidden Gem: Canal Saint-Martin. Perfect for a evening stroll and picnic, popular among young locals.
- Hidden Gem: Promenade Plantée (Coulée verte René-Dumont). A linear park built atop a historic elevated railway line, similar to NY's High Line.

## Seasonal Advice
- Best Time to Visit: Spring (April to June) and Fall (September to October) have mild weather and fewer tourists.
- Summer (July-August): Many local shops and bakeries close in August as Parisians go on vacation.

## Safety & Local Transport
- Safety: Be vigilant of pickpockets on Metro Line 1 and around major sights like the Eiffel Tower and Sacré-Cœur. Never sign petitions or buy bracelets from street vendors.
- Transport: Buy a Navigo Easy card for the Metro. Tickets are no longer sold as paper singles at many stations. Metro runs until 1:15 AM on weekdays and 2:15 AM on weekends.

# New York City Travel Guide

## Culture & Etiquette
- Fast Pace: New Yorkers walk fast. If you need to stop to look at a map or take a photo, step to the side of the sidewalk.
- Tipping: Tipping is standard practice in restaurants, bars, and taxis. Tip 18% to 22% of the pre-tax bill. For bartenders, $1 to $2 per drink is standard.
- Dining: Make reservations weeks in advance for popular restaurants using Resy or OpenTable.

## Food Recommendations
- Pizza: Joe's Pizza in Greenwich Village for a classic NYC slice. For sit-down, try Lombardi's (NoLIta) or Lucali (Brooklyn).
- Bagels: Russ & Daughters for bagels with lox and cream cheese. Ess-a-Bagel is also highly recommended.
- Delis: Katz's Delicatessen for the legendary pastrami sandwich. Be prepared to wait in line.

## Local Attractions & Hidden Gems
- High Line: An elevated park built on a historic freight rail line. Start at Hudson Yards and walk south to Chelsea.
- Central Park: Rent a bike or walk around the Bethesda Terrace and the Mall.
- Hidden Gem: Roosevelt Island Tramway. Use your MetroCard/OMNY to get a cheap, scenic aerial view of the East River and Manhattan skyline.
- Hidden Gem: The Morgan Library & Museum. J. Pierpont Morgan's majestic private library featuring three-story bookshelves and historic manuscripts.

## Seasonal Advice
- Best Time: Autumn (September to November) has comfortable temperatures.
- Holidays: December is magical with the Rockefeller Center tree and holiday windows, but highly crowded.
- Summer: July and August are very hot and the subway stations can feel like saunas.

## Safety & Local Transport
- Safety: NYC is generally safe for tourists. Stay alert in crowded areas (like Times Square) to avoid pickpockets. Avoid walking in dark, empty areas of parks at night.
- Transport: Use OMNY (contactless payment) to tap-and-go at any subway station using your phone or credit card. No need to buy a physical MetroCard. Subway runs 24/7.

# Tokyo Travel Guide

## Culture & Etiquette
- Handing Items: Use both hands when passing money, credit cards, or business cards.
- Tipping: Tipping is strictly non-existent and can be considered offensive. If you leave money behind, staff will chase you down to return it.
- Noise: Keep your voice low on public transit. Do not make phone calls on trains.
- Trash: There are almost no public trash cans. Carry a small plastic bag to store your trash until you return to your hotel, or discard items next to vending machines (for bottles/cans only).

## Food Recommendations
- Ramen: Ichiran or Afuri (known for Yuzu Shio Ramen). For a local feel, visit Omoide Yokocho (Memory Lane) near Shinjuku.
- Tsukiji Outer Market: Go early (7 AM) for fresh sushi, tamagoyaki (sweet omelet), and grilled seafood.
- Izakaya: Nonbei Yokocho (Drunkard's Alley) in Shibuya for tiny bars and grilled yakitori.
- Local Specialty: Try Monjayaki (savory pancake) in Tsukishima.

## Local Attractions & Hidden Gems
- Shibuya Crossing: Best viewed from the second floor of the Starbucks or the Shibuya Sky observation deck.
- Senso-ji Temple: Located in Asakusa, Tokyo's oldest temple. Go early morning or at night when it is illuminated.
- Hidden Gem: Yanaka District. One of the few areas that survived WWII, retaining old Tokyo vibes, traditional shops, and quiet streets.
- Hidden Gem: Todoroki Valley. A lush, wooden ravine hidden right inside Tokyo's Setagaya ward, featuring a quiet walking path and shrine.

## Seasonal Advice
- Cherry Blossom (Sakura) Season: Late March to early April. Parks like Shinjuku Gyoen and Ueno are gorgeous but crowded.
- Autumn Foliage: Late November to early December is fantastic with beautiful ginkgo trees.
- Summer: July and August are extremely humid and hot.

## Safety & Local Transport
- Safety: Tokyo is one of the safest cities in the world. However, beware of drink-spiking scams in nightlife districts like Roppongi and Kabukicho.
- Transport: Get a Suica or Pasmo IC Card. You can add them directly to your Apple Wallet. The subway shuts down around midnight.

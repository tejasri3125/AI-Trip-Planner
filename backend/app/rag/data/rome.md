# Rome Travel Guide

## Culture & Etiquette
- Dress Code: When visiting churches (especially St. Peter's Basilica and the Vatican Museums), both men and women must cover their shoulders and knees.
- Water: Do not buy plastic water bottles constantly. Rome has over 2,500 drinking fountains called "nasoni" providing free, cold, running spring water. Carry a reusable bottle.
- Coffee: Italians drink espresso standing up at the bar. If you sit at a table, you will pay a much higher table service fee (coperto). Never order a cappuccino after 11 AM.

## Food Recommendations
- Pasta: Try the Roman quadrumvirate of pastas: Carbonara, Cacio e Pepe, Amatriciana, and Gricia. Da Enzo al 29 in Trastevere is famous for these.
- Pizza: For thin, Roman-style pizza, visit Emma. For pizza al taglio (by the slice), visit Pizzarium near the Vatican.
- Gelato: Frigidarium or Gelateria del Teatro for fresh, artisanal gelato.

## Local Attractions & Hidden Gems
- Colosseum & Roman Forum: Buy tickets weeks in advance. The official reseller tickets sell out instantly.
- Trevi Fountain: Visit late at night (after 11 PM) or very early morning (before 7 AM) to experience it without crowds.
- Hidden Gem: The Aventine Keyhole. Look through the brass keyhole of the Priory of the Knights of Malta for a perfectly framed view of St. Peter's dome.
- Hidden Gem: Quartiere Coppedè. A small neighborhood designed in a whimsical, eclectic blend of Art Nouveau, Gothic, and Baroque architecture.

## Seasonal Advice
- Best Time: Spring (April-May) and Autumn (September-October) are ideal.
- Summer: July and August are scorching, often exceeding 38°C (100°F). Many local shops close in mid-August for the Ferragosto holiday.

## Safety & Local Transport
- Safety: Beware of pickpockets on Bus 64 (popular route from Termini to the Vatican) and at Termini Station. Don't accept "free" roses, bracelets, or help at ticket machines.
- Transport: Rome is best explored on foot. For public transport, you can buy a ticket on the MooneyGo app or tap your contactless card on buses and the metro.
